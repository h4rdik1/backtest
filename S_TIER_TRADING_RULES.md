# S-Tier Institutional Trading Rules (SMC)

This document outlines the exact sequence and conditions required for an "S-Tier" high-conviction trade setup, based on the **Double Sweep (HTF + LTF)** strategy. 

Patience is the core of this system. We do not chase price; we set limits at institutional zones and let the market manipulate into them.

---

## The "Pure S-Tier" Setup Sequence (Short Example)

For a trade to achieve S-Tier conviction, the following sequence must play out in order:

### 1. HTF Rejection (e.g., 4H Timeframe)
- **Identify HTF Order Block (OB) / FVG**: A clear origin of supply/demand.
- **Swing Failure Pattern (SFP)**: Price must push/wick *into* the OB, sweeping liquidity above an old swing high, and then **close back below** the OB.
- *Status:* `HTF Institutional Rejection Confirmed ✅`

### 2. LTF Structure Shift (e.g., 15M Timeframe)
- **Market Structure Break (MSB)**: Following the HTF SFP, price must forcefully break the recent LTF higher lows (for a short) or lower highs (for a long), proving that the HTF rejection triggered a real trend change.
- *Status:* `Structure Shift Confirmed ✅`

### 3. LTF OB Formation & Limit Placement
- **Identify the Origin**: The MSB must leave behind a clear unmitigated Order Block or FVG.
- **Set the Trap**: Place your Limit order inside this LTF OB. 
- *Status:* `Limit Entry Zone Identified ✅`

### 4. The Engineered Retrace (The Sweep)
- **Wait for the Bounce**: Do not chase the drop. Wait for price to naturally retrace back towards your LTF OB.
- **The Liquidity Grab**: Before tapping your limit, price must form some swing highs (Buy-Side Liquidity) just below or inside your OB.
- **The Final Sweep**: A 15M candle wicks *above* those swing highs (into your OB to fill your limit) and **closes back below** them.
- *Status:* `Final Liquidity Grab = S-Tier Confirmed ✅`

---

## Why the Final LTF Sweep makes it S-Tier:
1. **Clears Liquidity:** It takes out early retail shorts who placed stop-losses just above recent highs.
2. **Fills Smart Money:** Smart money (and your limit) uses the buy-stops of retail traders (who think it's a breakout) to fill their massive short positions.
3. **No Resistance Left:** With stops cleared and new "breakout" longs trapped, the price can reverse aggressively with no liquidity left to slow it down.

---

## Execution Rules & Discipline

- **Never enter before the candle close.** An SFP is a *wick* that closes back inside structure. If you enter while the candle is still forming, it could just be a true breakout.
- **Do not chase the price.** If price misses your limit and continues down, let it go. There will always be another setup. 
- **Invalidation (SL):** Always place your Stop Loss clearly above/below the HTF structure or the extreme of the OB to give the sweep room to breathe.
- **Take Profits (TP):**
  - **TP1:** Safe partial at the most recent Swing Low/High (usually 1.5R to 2R).
  - **TP2:** Full target at the next major liquidity pool.
